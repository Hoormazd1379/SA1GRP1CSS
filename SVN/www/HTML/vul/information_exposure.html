Vulnerabilities

Information Exposure

Statistics

  • Type: Class

  • CWE Number: 200

  • Applicable Platforms: Language-independent

  • Likelyhood of exploit: High

Overview

Information exposure is the intentional or unintentional disclosure of information to someone that is not explicitly authorised to have access to that information.
A program may accidentally reveal valuable data to a user. This could be private messages or program configuration files that are supposed to be private and which could give attackers a way into the system.

Information Exposure occurs through numerous different ways.
For Example:

  • Information Exposure Through Sent Data

  • Exposure of Sensitive Data Through Data Queries

  • Information Exposure through an Error Message

Consequences

Loss of confidentiality is the most common consequence, as data is often disclosed. This often has security implications.

Detection

Regular error-checking methods will identify this vulnerability: both dynamic analysis and static analysis.

There also exist several context-specific weakness analyzers that will detect this issue in a source code.

Mitigation

In your system define "safe" areas where trust boundaries can be unambiguously drawn. Do not allow sensitive data to go outside of the trust boundary and always be careful when interfacing with a compartment outside of the safe area.
