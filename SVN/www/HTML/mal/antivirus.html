Antivirus software is a program used to protect users' devices from malicious codes.
Antivirus softwares usually run in the background of your device, scanning and checking every file that is being opened.
Antivirus apps, may also check files that contain viruses, the moment they are stored-downloaded in your device and prevent you from opening the infected file.
All these happen without you being aware of what's going on in the background.




Most antivirus programs would be better described as anti-malwares since they are often designed to protect against Worms, Trojans and other types of malicious code.
There are also antiviruses designed for browser security. It’s possible for malware, hackers and other cyber criminals to exploit weaknesses in web browsers.
The major Web Browsers (Google Chrome, Firefox, Opera, Safari) also include a number of other security features, such as pop-up blockers and phishing detectors, which can warn you if a website appears to be a fraud.
Firewall is the first protection layer of your device against unauthorized access to your PC.
It works in the background all the time monitoring traffic to (and, in some cases, from) your computer and blocks certain ports while allows specific programs and services to communicate with your PC.
Protecting from phishing frauds takes a two-pronged effort, with anti-spam software filtering out unsolicited emails and web browser security features protecting against known fraudulent sites.
Last but not least there, exists an anti-spam software, which blocks unwanted or unsolicited email from reaching your email's inbox.
