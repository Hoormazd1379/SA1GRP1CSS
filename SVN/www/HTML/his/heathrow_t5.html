Heathrow T5 IT problems

"Apparently the computer software told the baggage people that the flight had taken off. So everyone in the plane just watched as all their suitcases were taken back into the terminal instead of being loaded on."

After long time spending on planning and constructing the Terminal 5 of the London Heathrow Airport, no one had doubts about the big future of the T5 and its success. But sometimes, little problems may cause a lot of financial damage.



What Happened

During the first days on its opening, due to IT problems and lack of testing their programs, the Terminal 5 faced a "national humiliation". Once the staff were ready to work, some workers reported to not be able to log on to the computer system, and others reported the problem to access the Resource Management System, which permits to loar or unload flights. Even with the confusion, the working members continued to add luggage to the system, which after a limit (designed to hold 12,000 bags an hour) broke, and the airline was forced to stop the check-in of all flights to solve the issue of the backlog bags.
