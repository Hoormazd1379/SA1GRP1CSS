Ariane 5 Flight 501

1996 - French Guiana

What happened: June 4, 1966 was a day of great disappointment for the ESA. On this day, in fact, the inaugural voyage of the Ariane 5 rocket was planned, unfortunately something went wrong. The use of the internal reference system of the previous model (A4) on the current A5 caused an arithmetic overflow that then led to the destruction of the rocket. The losses in economic terms are estimated at around 400 million for the material value of the rocket and 8 billion for the development work that lasted 10 years.
