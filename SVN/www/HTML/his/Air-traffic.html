Air Traffic Control in LA Airport

The air traffic control has the important responsibility of informing aircraft pilots about relevant information regarding weather, routes, the distance between other airplanes, and more. Failing to communicate with aircraft pilots promptly could result in catastrophe mostly in finance field. On September 14, 2004, at 5 P.M. air traffic control at the LA airport lost voice communication with approximately 800 airplanes being tracked in the southwestern United States and many planes were headed towards each other. So what happened? The primary voice communication system shut down unexpectedly. The computer had to examine a large number of air routes to "deconflict the aircraft with lower-altitude flights". The process "used aq large amount of available memory and interruped the computer's other flight-processing functions".
