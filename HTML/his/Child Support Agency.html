The Child Support Agency-EDS program Failure

Launched on 5 April 1993, The Child Support Agency tried to implement the Child Support Act of 1991, which was passed to reform the legislation regarding payments for domestic maintenance in New Zealand. This Act enabled the parents to apply to the Inland Revenue Department and receive, if they had encounter all the conditions, child support from the Government. The Child Support Agency had the important role of calculating, collecting, enforcing and transferring the payment to the parents. The CSA's old computer system was not enought to do this operation, so they decided to use a new system, bought from EDS (Electronic Data System), but the problem was that while implementing this new system, the CSA was undergoing reconstruction. The Supervisors didn't actually care too much about all the flaws and holes in the new Software, and the program was anyway installed.

The consequences

The System crashed since the two pieces of software were incompatible, and some inevitable errors showed up. table those errors results in over 1.9 million overpaid people, and more or less 700,000 people underpaied. The problems didn't stop there, here is a complete list:

  • Families were underpaied
  • More than half of the data got stuck on the system
  • 1.9 million people overpaied
  • 700,000 families underpaied
  • A total backlog of 250,000 cases
  • 7$ billion in uncollected child support payments
  • Only one third of the applications for child maintenace have been processed
  • Only 4% of the applicants got the right amount
